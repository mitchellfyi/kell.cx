Building Your Tracking System

You have three options, each with tradeoffs:

Option 1: Manual (Free, High Effort)

Create a spreadsheet. Check each competitor once a week. Record changes.

Works if: You have 3-5 competitors max and discipline to check weekly.

Fails when: You get busy (spoiler: you will). Important changes slip through because you checked on Tuesday but pricing changed on Wednesday.

Option 2: DIY Automation (Low Cost, High Setup)

Write scrapers that check pages daily. Store history. Diff for changes.

Stack I'd recommend:

  • Playwright or Puppeteer for scraping
  • Cron jobs or GitHub Actions for scheduling
  • Simple JSON storage with git history
  • Slack/email notifications for changes

Works if: You're technical and enjoy building internal tools.

Fails when: Sites change their structure, you hit rate limits, you don't have time to maintain scrapers that inevitably break.

Option 3: Use a Service (Low Effort, Has Cost)

Pay someone else to handle the infrastructure. Get a daily digest.

Works if: Your time is worth more than the subscription cost.

Fails when: The service doesn't track your specific competitors or signals.

My take: Start with Option 1 to validate what you actually care about. If you find yourself consistently checking the same things, either automate it or pay for a service. Time spent refreshing competitor pricing pages is time not spent building your product.

The "So What?" Test

Before adding any new signal to your tracking, ask: "If this changes, would I do anything differently?"

If the answer is no, don't track it. Competitive intelligence should inform decisions, not satisfy curiosity.
