Detailed Breakdown by Tool

Cursor

cursor.com

Hobby (Free) $0
Pro $20/month
Business $40/seat/month

Cursor's free tier includes 2,000 completions and 50 slow premium requests per month. Pro unlocks unlimited completions and 500 fast premium requests. Business adds centralized billing, admin dashboard, and enforced privacy mode.

GitHub Copilot

github.com/features/copilot

Free (students/OSS) $0
Individual $10/month
Business $19/seat/month
Enterprise $39/seat/month

Copilot's free tier is limited to students, teachers, and open source maintainers. Enterprise adds custom model fine-tuning, SAML SSO, and audit logs. The most widely adopted AI coding tool with deep IDE integration.

Windsurf (Codeium)

codeium.com

Individual (Free) $0
Pro $15/month
Teams $38/seat/month

Codeium's free tier is notably generous—unlimited basic completions with no waitlist. Pro adds faster completions and more context. Windsurf is their new IDE product with deeper integration. Recently raised $150M at $1.25B valuation.

Devin (Cognition AI)

cognition.ai

Core $500/month
Enterprise Custom pricing

Devin is a different category—an autonomous AI software engineer rather than a coding assistant. The $500/month price reflects that it's designed to complete entire tasks independently. Includes unlimited ACUs (Autonomous Compute Units) on the Core plan.

Replit AI

replit.com

Free $0
Replit Core $25/month
Teams Pro $40/seat/month

Replit bundles AI with their cloud development environment. The Core plan includes advanced AI features plus always-on deployments. Best value if you're already using Replit for hosting.

Price Categories

Best Free Tier

Codeium/Windsurf wins here with unlimited basic completions at no cost. Continue.dev is fully open source (free forever) but requires you to bring your own API keys. GitHub Copilot's free tier is restricted to students and open source maintainers.

Best Value for Individuals

Sourcegraph Cody at $9/month is the cheapest paid option. GitHub Copilot at $10/month remains the most battle-tested choice with the widest IDE support.

Best for Teams

GitHub Copilot Business at $19/seat offers the best combination of price and features for teams. Supermaven at $20/seat is competitive if you want faster completions.

Enterprise/Custom Pricing

Augment Code, Poolside, and Amazon Q Developer's enterprise tiers don't publish prices. Expect $30-100/seat/month depending on features and volume. Devin's $500/month is usage-based for a fundamentally different product.

Hidden Costs to Consider

  • Usage limits: Many "unlimited" plans cap premium model access. Cursor's Pro plan limits fast requests to 500/month.
  • Model tiers: Free tiers often use smaller, faster models. Paid plans unlock GPT-4, Claude, etc.
  • Context windows: Enterprise plans typically offer larger context for better codebase understanding.
  • Compliance: SOC 2, HIPAA, or custom data retention may require enterprise contracts.
  • IDE lock-in: Cursor requires using their editor. Others work across IDEs.

Recommendations by Use Case

Hobbyist / Side Projects

Start with Codeium's free tier. It's genuinely unlimited for basic completions. Upgrade to GitHub Copilot ($10) when you want better quality.

Solo Developer / Freelancer

Cursor Pro at $20/month if you're willing to use their editor—the experience is notably better than plugins. Otherwise, GitHub Copilot at $10 for IDE flexibility.

Startup (5-20 devs)

GitHub Copilot Business at $19/seat for the safest choice. Consider Cursor Business ($40/seat) if your team prefers the integrated experience.

Enterprise (50+ devs)

Get quotes from GitHub, Tabnine, and Amazon Q. Compliance, audit logs, and custom model training matter more than per-seat pricing at this scale.

Trying to Replace Junior Devs

Devin at $500/month is the only product explicitly designed for autonomous task completion. ROI depends on whether it can actually handle your tickets independently.

Our Take

The market is converging on $10-20/month for individuals and $20-40/seat for teams. Differentiation is shifting from "has AI" to specific capabilities: better context understanding, codebase-aware suggestions, autonomous task completion.

We expect continued price pressure as competition intensifies. The free tiers will stay generous to drive adoption. Enterprise pricing will become more usage-based as AI costs drop.

For most developers, GitHub Copilot remains the default choice—it's cheap, well-integrated, and good enough. For power users who want the best experience, Cursor is worth the premium.
